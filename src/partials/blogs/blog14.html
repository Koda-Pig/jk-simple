OK this was a cool one. I really enjoy challenges like this - a good balance of complexity, and not a problem best solved using only gradients (which gets a bit boring imo). I used a dotted border to create the 3 top dots (html element) and single bottom dot (body element). Used a radial gradient to draw the half circle, and fill in the orange background.

I want to take a bit of a deeper look at the radial gradient in this solution though, as it was revealed that there was a lot I could have done to optimize this. Looking at the background shorthand code again: We have here:

  • A circular radial gradient (1q) positioned at 50% on the x-axis and 0 on the y-axis. Note that this could be shortened by one character to
  • A gradient with 2 colors (orange and black) and hard stops at 40px (5ch) and 80px (5pc).
  • A background size of 300% on the x-axis and 109% on the y-axis.
  • A background position of 50% on the x-axis and 111% on the y-axis

This is a decent illustration of the sheer power of gradients in CSS. They are an essential tool in any CSS Battlers toolbelt.
